Introduction

I’m a software developer based in the Salt Lake City valley area. I currently work at Huntsman Cancer Institute in the Clinical Trials Office as their Activation Administrator. My work in this role has involved developing a database and system to track, and distribute, information about study opportunities and statuses to our teams. I am also developing tools for communication with companies to minimize redundancy of work. Additionally, I interface with pharmaceutical representatives answering questions about our research facilities and capabilities. I have a B.S. and M.S. (Psychology, and Experimental Psychology respectively) and am working on my B.S. in Software Development which I expect to complete before the end of 2022.

When I’m not writing code, or thinking about writing code, you might find me spending quality time with my wife and two dogs (Carter & Odin) or playing board games with friends or family.

Personal Statement

I am currently looking for a full-time developer role that will allow me to spend more significant amounts of time writing code and increasing my skills as a software engineer. I currently work independently but would love to work with a small team in the future. In terms of tools, I’m language and technology agnostic. I’m most experienced with the technologies that I have listed as used most often (see resume section). However, I very much enjoy learning new things and thinking about different ways of solving problems. An appropriate tool for the use case is more aligned with my philosophy.

At work, and let’s be honest at home too, I’m a problem solver down to my core. I love the challenge of seeing a question or problem and coming up with a creative and efficient solution to explain the question or overcome the obstacle. I learn new skills quickly and independently without much trouble. Interpersonally, I get a lot of gratification from being able to help my colleagues when I can. When it comes to technology and development, I frequently put extra time during my free hours in to learn new things just because I enjoy understanding and growing my skills.

I believe I would be an excellent addition to any development team in terms of productivity, especially given a couple of weeks to polish my execution and get the feel for the workflow. I think that how fast I pick up a role and become valuable would likley exceed expectations. My current bosses would likely attest to that. My best personality fit would be with a team or group of equally passionate, friendly, and curious people.

Background

Not everyone needs the background, so feel free to skip, but if you are here for the long story read on friends! My first experience with coding was around 2011, in college. It started with a book bought from Barnes and Noble called C++ in 48 hours, or 24 hours maybe, something like that. At the time I had very little career knowledge about the technology industry, so I didn’t realize that writing code and creating solutions was something that someone might really do for a living. I was in college so I knew people were majoring in it, but it didn’t occur to me what they might do after in practice. I was from rural Louisiana and had only just gotten my first computer maybe a year earlier. But I was curious as always and decided to give it a look. I dabbled with the language for a while but only in a for the sake of exploration sense. Without much direction for how I might use this skill in my other endeavors. As I moved along in my traditional studies the time that I spent on the side project of code dabbling decreased to essentially nothing other than a passing thought.

Some lingering interest however remained, during my research time in my undergraduate and graduate studies the time that I spent dabbling in code made me appreciate need for technology to work with and even collect our research data. My exploration did not deepen further however as my path at the time seemed marked out. The plan at the time was to go on to become PhD level clinical psychologist. However, after working in the mental health field for a time attempting to prepare for this next step, I found that this wasn’t the correct plan for me. My more curious and problem-solving nature became burned out and under stimulated in a clinical environment.

I preferred the research process, the questions, the data, the statistics, and the technology than anything that I found in the clinical space. In 2019 I pivoted back to the research field, finding full time employment at HCI, where I also began pursing coding and technology more steadily. I started with Python, something that had heavy applications in data science (something more familiar to me) and slowly branched out into the web technologies (JS, HTML, CSS, Django) over time. I’ve been working on a second bachelor's degree this time in Software Development (which I’m finishing up soon), the curriculum requires a language focus of Java and includes a little C++.

My current full-time job was a new position the role and duties of which I’ve gotten the opportunity to outline myself. Ultimately, I’ve developed a database structure the team currently uses to track and distribute early startup information. Along with front end tools for our users. At this time Microsoft access is used as their database platform but depending on my tenure and the capabilities of my replacement we may be migrating over to PostgreSQL. Currently, I’m working on a multifaceted startup data management system that utilizes PostgreSQL, JS, HTML, CSS, and Django. I hope to roll out pieces of the interface on my projects page soon.
